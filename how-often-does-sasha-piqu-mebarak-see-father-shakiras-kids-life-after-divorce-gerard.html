How Often Can Gerard Piqué See His Sons, Milan And Sasha Piqué Mebarak?

Gerard Piqué and Shakira experienced one of the most publicized breakups of 2022. The duo has been subjected to relentless media scrutiny, which has only intensified as Shakira's star has risen on the strength of her numerous musical albums.

The specifics of Piqué and Shakira's custody arrangement, including the frequency of his visitation, have been reported by a Spanish newspaper.

According to La Vanguardia, one of the main reasons Shakira and Piqué spent so much time in court was to work out a custody agreement for their children. The publication reported that Piqué has unrestricted access to his children and that he and Shakira will split the costs of any trips to see them in Miami.

Piqué is also free to visit and take the kids out on any ten days of the month. They may spend some time with his relatives in Miami, or he could take them on a journey that includes a visit to Spain to meet his father's side of the family. Piqué's relocation to Miami would result in a 50/50 custody arrangement.

In recent weeks, Shakira and her family finalized their relocation to Miami, and her children began attending classes in a new country. Shakira made a serious and forthright request that the media stop prying into her family's private life.

“I beg you to abstain from following them to the entrance or exit of their school, waiting for them at the door of our home, or following them to their after-school activities, as it has happened every day in Barcelona with the purpose of taking photographs or for a better rating,” she wrote.

Gerard Piqué Is Committed To Caring For His Kids, Especially After The Divorce From Shakira

Gerard Piqué, who split up from international superstar Shakira, is putting his attention squarely on his children.

First to speak publicly after his divorce from the singer in June 2022, the former soccer player told the Spanish newspaper El Pais that he is still focused on keeping his eyes on their two young sons, Sasha, 8, and Milan, 10.

"Everyone has their responsibility to do what's best for their kids," he said, as translated from Spanish to English.

"It's about protecting them," he added. "That's the job of all parents with their kids. That's what I'm focused on, and that's my role as a father."

Shakira confirmed her romance with the soccer star in March 2011, and she later revealed their split in June of the same year. Since then, Piqué has made his relationship with Clara Chia Martí public on Instagram, and Shakira has published three songs that appear to be about the breakup.

The most recent is "TQG," a collaboration with fellow Colombian artist Karol G in which they sing about growing stronger as they watch an ex-partner go on.

In a September interview with ELLE, Shakira expressed similar feelings regarding her own children, saying that she was doing all in her power to "conceal" the situation from them. In fact, when Sasha was born, it was evident that Shakira wanted to take time off to focus all her efforts on her family.

"I try to do it and to protect them because that's my number one mission in life," she said. The singer and the former soccer player settled custody of their sons in November 2022.

"Our sole objective is to provide our children with the utmost security and protection and hope that they can continue with their lives in a safe and calm environment," the former couple shared to People at the time. "We appreciate that their privacy will be respected."
