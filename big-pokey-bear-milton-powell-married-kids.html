Big Pokey Bear’s Life and Career

Big Pokey was born Milton Powell on November 29, 1974, in Houston, Texas, U.S.

He led a quiet life until the 1990s when he started to draw attention with his professional rapping career with Houston producer DJ Screw. Big Pokey contributed to Screw’s Freestyle which had a long-lasting effect in Houston and on the American music scene.

This marked the beginning of his spending decades contributing to the music industry.

Moreso, he was a founding member of The “Screwed Up Click” (S.U.C).

Pokey’s first full-length album was Hardest Pit in the Litter. It was released on May 18, 1999, and it peaked at #72 on the US Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart. It also reached Billboard US Heatseekers Albums.

What Happened to Big Pokey Bear?

Big Pokey Bear died on Sunday, June 18, 2023, at the age of 45. He died after collapsing on a stage while performing in Texas. CPR was admonished and the rapper was rushed to the hospital shortly before his demise.

His death came as a shock to many but there is a bit of a consolation that the unique and highly talented artist died while doing what he truly loved.
