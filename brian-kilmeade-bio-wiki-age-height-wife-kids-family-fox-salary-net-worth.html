Brian Kilmeade Biography

Brian Kilmeade is an American television and radio reporter currently working for Fox News alongside Ainsley Earhardt and Steve Doocy. He co-hosts Fox & Friends, Fox’s morning show, and he hosts the Fox News Radio program The Brian Kilmeade Show. Kilmeade is also a co-author of fiction and non-fiction books.

Brian Kilmeade Journalist

Kilmeade started his career on Channel One News, a daily national high school television news program as a correspondent. He then worked as an anchor and host for KHSC-TV in Ontario, California.

He additionally co-hosted The Jim Brown Show on XTRA-AM (KLSD-XTRA 1360 Fox Sports San Diego), an all-sports radio network. In 1993, he joined Jim Brown as part of the announcing team for the inaugural Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC 1) event, conducting post-fight interviews. In 1994, he shifted into the play-by-play role for UFC 2 and UFC 3.

Beginning in 1997, Kilmeade was a freelance sports anchor for WVIT (NBC) in Hartford, Connecticut. He was a sideline reporter for the MSG Network, where he covered the New York/New Jersey MetroStars, which is a major league soccer team based in Giants Stadium

He later worked as a feature reporter and anchor for Newsport TV, where he hosted Newsport Journal, a daily magazine show for the national sports network and also anchored Scoreboard Central, which is a live half-hour general sports program. Read also about George Noory.

Brian Kilmeade Fox News

Kilmeade joined Fox News in 1997. After joining Fox News, he was soon named co-host of Fox & Friends, where he still thrives today. In 2000, the show powered its way to the top spot in the morning cable TV and remained there till day.

He has the privilege of hosting his self-titled nationally syndicated radio show, on Fox News Talk from 9-12 noon. The show broke the top 20 as one of the most powerful on the radio by “Talkers Magazine”.

During his tenure at Fox News, Kilmeade has developed the ability to interview and provide insight into newsmakers, celebrities, politicians, and athletes.

 Kilmeade Radio
