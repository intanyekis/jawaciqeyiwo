Who is Brock Anderson? Arn Andersons son joins AEW as Cody Rhodes tag partner

 Posted on July 8, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  679 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The latest episode of All Elite Wrestling also known as AEW, was all over the internet as fans thoroughly enjoyed the brand new matches between wrestlers like Brian Cage, Powerhouse Hobbs, Hangman Page, The Young Bucks and Brandon Cutler along with Eddie Kingston, PAC, and Penta.

The latest event dubbed as AEW Dynamite also introduced the fans to WWE Hall of Famer Arn Anderson’s son Brock Anderson and it was revealed that he will be making his debut next week as Cody Rhodes’ tag partner. Brock Anderson soon reserved a spot on the weekend trending topics mainly due to the way he dressed up for the occasion. Speaking of wrestling, wrestlers like Andrade, Drew McIntyre, Lars Sullivan and others made a lot of noise for their recent WWE matches.

Who is Brock Anderson?

The son of wrestling legend Arn Anderon, Brock Anderson made his very first appearance in front of the cameras when he was spotted with his father in the ring for his introduction. The young wrestler will team up with Cody Rhodes against wrestlers QT Marshall and Aaron Solow. Tony Schiavone, who hosted the coveted event, announced that Brock will be making his AEW debut in next week's matches. Brock Anderson has no prior experience of wrestling except he has been training under at Cody’s Nightmare Factory for quite some time now. Brock Anderson might be making his wrestling debut soon but he has been often spotted in the crowd at AEW Dynamite and Dark on several occasions. 

The official Twitter account of AEW also shared the details about Brock’s debut saying, “The next generation of the Anderson Family makes his debut next week on #AEWDynamite when @CodyRhodes teams with the son of the #Enforcer@TheArnShow, #BrockAnderson against @realmmarshall1 & @aaronsolow.” Speaking of Arn Anderson, he is widely regarded as one of the finest tag team wrestlers in history. The retired professional wrestler signed to All Elite Wrestling (AEW) is also a four-time NWA/WCW World Television Champion and he was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame as a member of the Four Horsemen in March 2012.
